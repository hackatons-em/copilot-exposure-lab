# Startup Thesis

## Thesis

Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Studio make old Microsoft 365 permission problems more visible and more dangerous. Companies already have overshared SharePoint sites, broad OneDrive links, stale guest access, missing sensitivity labels, risky Power Platform connectors, and unmanaged agents. Copilot and agents can make those exposures easier to discover or act on.

Copilot Exposure Lab helps security and M365 teams answer:

> Before we expand Copilot, what sensitive data or agent action could be exposed, and what exactly do we fix first?

## Why now
- Copilot is moving from pilots to broader deployment.
- Security teams need evidence before approving AI access to internal data.
- Microsoft-native controls are powerful but spread across Purview, Defender, SharePoint, Entra, Sentinel, and Copilot Studio.
- Buyers need scenario-based evidence and proof-of-fix, not just posture dashboards.

## Wedge
A controlled **Copilot exposure drill**:
1. Build permission and resource graph.
2. Run realistic scenarios.
3. Generate evidence-backed findings.
4. Map fixes to Microsoft-native controls.
5. Rerun to prove exposure is fixed.

## Expansion path
Start with SharePoint/OneDrive/Copilot readiness. Expand to Copilot Studio agents, Power Platform connectors, Teams/Outlook, Purview/Defender/Sentinel integrations, custom RAG, Google Workspace, Slack, Salesforce, ServiceNow, and broader AI exposure management.
